Teascape: An iOS and Android Tea Store App

DEFINE

The Problem

Teascape needs to be able to reach out to more customers. Many shops are moving to mobile apps as consumers are more likely to shop
at stores that have mobile access than those that do not. A mobile app also allows consumers to be able to interact with your brand in
the comfort of their own home or on the go.

Key Differences

As a UI designer, developing a native app for either iOS or Android will mean taking a close look at the
guidelines and designing an interface that is faithful to them. Android is governed by Material Design
and is characterized by its depth and planes that call back to how paper stacked on each other looks
and feels. iOS, on the other hand is more flat and follows Apple’s iOS Human Interface Guidelines.

A Few Notes

I noticed that when I started to get the design ready to prototype that I was missing a few screens,
like edit/error screens, the purchase order screen and another timer ending screen.

Sounds & Physical Feedback

Haptic feedback refers to the different vibration patterns you feel when you interact with a device.
It’s what you feel when you receive a text or enter the wrong information into a field.
So for this app I decided that I would like to include some sounds as well as vibrations.

Password Error

When the user accidentally enters the wrong password they’ll feel a sharp vibration from their
phone alerting them of the error.

Steep Timer

The Steep Timer will emit a slight positive sounding ding when the user clicks “Begin Timer” so
they know the apps begun the countdown.

Steep Timer Done

Once the timer has reached 0:00 an alarm will sound as well as a vibration. It’s important to try
and grab their attention in any way possible as if you over steep tea you’ll get a bitter mess that’s not enjoyable at all.

Place Order

I think that it’s important when a user is on the final stage of checking out that there be some
sort of noise or vibration feedback. I want there to be a “Ding!” of some sort and a light, positive
vibration when the user taps “Place Order” before the screen loads to the next one. That way there’s no question whether they’ve hit it or not.

THOUGHTS

Retrospective

As I’ve taken time to reflect on this project I now realize there are a few things I’d of done differently.
I think it would have been nice to include a means to rate the teas and see what other people’s opinions were.
I also think it would have been interesting to maybe include a section for articles about tea so that anyone who is
new to loose leaf tea could learn more about steeping techniques and ways to mix different teas.

The other thing is after I’d tested my prototypes I noticed that there wasn’t a way for users to search for a tea to steep on the steep timer page.
That needs to be implemented.

Ultimately I am happy with the way this project has turned out. I also took a lot of feedback from
my mentor on how to improve the UI portion of the app. I implemented more colors, icons and better consistency throughout the app.

High Fidelity Wireframes

After receiving critiques from multiple users I then tweaked my wireframes based on their feedback.
One of the bigger issues that came up was that I was using red in places that did not need it.
I wanted it to be a warning color for things like wrong password and credit card error information.
I replaced the red in those areas with a blue. Another issue was that I did not keep the same language for “Sign Up” and “Login”
and was using other words like “Register” and “Sign In” and I needed to be more consistent as to not confuse users.
