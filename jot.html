Jot: A Note Taking App

OVERVIEW

Objective:

Build a Note Taking App with Dieter Rams “Less, But Better,” in mind.

Problem Statement:

The client is urgently requesting that I design a simple note taking app.
They expect that I design with “less is more” in mind and focus on the functionality of the app.

Challenge::

To deliver high-fidelity wireframes for 5 screens so the client can pitch the concept to their investors.

MY APPROACH

Research & Analysis:

I looked up similar simple apps like Samsung Notes and Google Keep.
The main thing I noticed about both of these are that they’re very simple, clean with a primary white color scheme.

Design & Wireframes:

I first started with brainstorming the look of the logo.
I wanted it to be a bit sketchy and organic. My first iterations were made on my iPad using
my Apple Pen on Procreate. I later translated these to Adobe Illustrator and started refining it.
My final version loses the squiggle line up under the word “Jot.” I felt like it made the logo less
professional. I did keep the pen, I feel like it adds some personality to the logo without looking too childish.

STYLE GUIDE

I immediately set forth designing the splash login screen and set the general mood of the app around that using cool blue’s and hot pinks.
I wanted both these colors to contrast with each other and provide a look that was comfortable and inclusive.

HIGH-FIDELITY WIREFRAMES

I wanted my dashboard to show all the notes on the phone that the user has posted.
They can scroll through to find the one’s they need. You can click on a note to enlarge it and edit it.

RETROSPECTIVE

What went well?

What went well? Being able to design an app in a short amount of time that I am very proud of.
Going outside of my comfort zone with colors that I don’t normally use together.
What did not go well? At first I designed the home screen with tiny text and it looked like it
would’ve fit a desktop type layout. I went back and fixed this by making text much larger, more
spaced out and a better color that fit with the general theme of the app.

What can be improved?

It would be interesting to see how far I could push this design if there
were no strict constraints with time spent and designing for “less, but better” in mind.
