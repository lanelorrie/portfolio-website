Pachira: A Money Saving App for Android

OVERVIEW

Objective:

Help people save money efficiently in preparation for a big purchase or goal.

Challenge:

Saving money can often feel like a stressful task, especially if you feel like you have to alter your spending or current way of living.

Solution:

Rewarding users for meeting their savings goals! I added some levels of gamification
in the app to keep things interesting. This helps users via “intrinsic motivation” where a
person is moved to act for the fun or challenge. It can help users to achieve their goals
and to overcome any negative association that they may have with budgeting and saving money.

PROJECT BRIEF

Who:

This tool is for people who want to save money, quickly, for a particular reason.
For example, a wedding, big trip, or new car. The users are not new to technology but do not
like working with their finances. So, they require that the information they receive via
the tool is accessible, and clearly tells them what they can do to better save.

What:

A responsive website, web app, and/or mobile app allowing all data on
income and expenses to be recorded easily, on the go, and from a variety of devices.
The tool displays data on the user’s finances (how much money they spend and on what),
and tells them what they can do to cut costs and save money in a certain amount of time.

When:

Users will utilize the tool in the period before a planned expense.
During this period, they will input data when spending and receiving money so they can
view accurate information on their finances and dynamic information on what they can do
to save more money.

Where:

The tool can be used from anywhere and using any device to ensure income and outgoings are easily recorded.

Why:

Saving money can be really hard, especially when you don’t have a long time to do so.
By providing personalized information on how a user can save based on their actual finances and
making the achievement of goals fun.

TEST ANALYSIS

PAIN POINT

  • Active and Inactive text input fields
  • “Go Back” being a redundant button for an Android
  • Transactions on income and expenses tab
  • Fingerprint icon too large
  • Cash Flow isn’t immediately recognized

PRIORITY

RECOMMENDATION

  • The labels are too dark. Maybe put label in box in inactive. Then transition it to the top when clicked.
  • Consider just keeping the bottom nav back button as having it and “Go Back” button is very redundant.
  • Place your income transactions on the income tab and leave all your outgoing transactions just for the expenses tab.
  • Consider slimming it down.
  • Maybe adding some text to explain what each line means.
